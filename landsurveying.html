Surveying FAQs

Answers to common questions about our land surveying services.

How long does a typical survey take?

Survey duration depends on property size, terrain, and survey type. Most residential boundary surveys are completed within 2-5 working days, while larger or more complex projects may take 1-2 weeks.

What documents do I need to provide for a survey?

To begin your survey, we typically require:

  • Title deed or land ownership documents
  • Any existing survey plans or diagrams
  • Identification documents
  • Location sketch or directions to the property

How much does a land survey cost?

Survey fees vary based on property size, location, terrain complexity, and survey type. Residential boundary surveys typically start from UGX 500,000, while topographic and construction surveys are quoted based on project specifics. Contact us for a detailed estimate tailored to your property.

Do I need a survey if I already have a title deed?

While a title deed proves ownership, a survey provides the physical evidence of your boundaries on the ground. We recommend a survey whenever you're buying, selling, developing, or if there are any boundary questions with neighbors.

How long is a survey valid?

A properly conducted survey doesn't expire, but we recommend updating surveys if there have been significant changes to the property or surrounding area, or if more than 5-10 years have passed since the last survey.
